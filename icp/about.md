# ICP: About Page
**URL:** `https://www.avenuelocks.com/about/`
**Template:** Custom
**Page Goal:** Build trust and humanize the brand for visitors who are evaluating Avenue Locksmith before hiring.

---

## Who Is Coming to This Page?

### Primary Visitor: The Evaluator / Trust-Checker (65%)
- **Who they are:** Someone who found Avenue Locksmith via search or referral and is doing due diligence before calling
- **Device:** Mix of desktop and mobile
- **Urgency:** Low-to-moderate — not an emergency, they're choosing a vendor
- **Pain:** "I've heard of locksmith scams. I want to know who I'm actually dealing with."
- **What wins them:** Real photos of the team, business story, license mention, community connection
- **What they're looking for:** Evidence this is a real, local, legitimate business — not a call center

### Secondary Visitor: Returning Customer (20%)
- **Who they are:** Past customer who wants to verify they have the right company, or to share the link with a friend
- **Device:** Mobile
- **Behavior:** Quick scan — confirm business name, phone, location
- **What wins them:** Consistent branding, same phone number, recognizable brand

### Tertiary: Journalist / Directory Researcher (15%)
- **Who they are:** Local business directory, local news, or review site researcher
- **Behavior:** Reading for business details
- **What wins them:** Clear, factual business information, contact details

---

## Pain Points This Page Must Resolve

1. "Is this a real local business or a shell company?"
2. "Who are the people behind this? Can I trust them in my home?"
3. "How long have they been in business? Are they experienced?"
4. "Are they truly licensed and insured?"
5. "Do they actually care about their community or are they just in it for the money?"

## Content Writer Notes
- This page is about TRUST above all else — every paragraph should add a trust signal
- Use first-person "we" — this is a people page, not a services page
- Real photos of real people will outperform any amount of copy — if available, use them
- The "not a call center" message is critical for NYC — many "locksmith companies" are actually national aggregators that dispatch unknown contractors
- Keep it honest and human — don't use corporate buzzwords
- Length: 500–700 words — substantive but not padded
