# ICP: Neighborhood Pages (/locksmith-near-me/[neighborhood]/)
**URL Pattern:** `https://www.avenuelocks.com/locksmith-near-me/[neighborhood-slug]/`
**Total Pages:** 50 neighborhood pages + 1 hub page
**Template:** C — Neighborhood Page
**Page Goal:** Capture hyper-local "locksmith near me" intent and convert to phone calls.

---

## Who Is Coming to These Pages?

### Primary Visitor: Hyper-Local Searcher (65%)
- **Who they are:** Someone IN that neighborhood right now or very recently, searching "locksmith near me" or "locksmith [neighborhood name]"
- **Device:** 85%+ mobile
- **Urgency:** Moderate to high — often a same-day need (lockout, rekey, security upgrade)
- **Key insight:** They chose this result because it mentions THEIR neighborhood specifically — they want LOCAL
- **Pain:** "I want a locksmith who actually serves MY area, not someone who drives 45 min and charges travel fees"
- **What wins them:** Their neighborhood name in the headline, mention of local streets or landmarks, explicit response time to that area

### Secondary Visitor: Research Phase (25%)
- **Who they are:** Resident of that neighborhood researching locksmiths proactively
- **Device:** Mixed
- **Urgency:** Low — saving a number for later, or planning a lock upgrade
- **What wins them:** Reviews from "other neighborhood residents" feel, range of services, pricing transparency

### Tertiary Visitor: Google Local Search Confirmer (10%)
- **Who they are:** Found the Google Business Profile or a map listing, clicked through to this page to verify legitimacy
- **Urgency:** Variable
- **What wins them:** Consistent NAP (Name, Address, Phone), reviews, looks professional

---

## The Golden Rule for Neighborhood Pages

**Every neighborhood page must feel like it was written for THAT neighborhood — not copy-pasted.**

Avoid thin content. Each page must have unique local signals:
- Mention specific streets, landmarks, or features of that neighborhood
- Reference the ZIP code(s) served
- Mention neighboring areas (with internal links)
- Response time estimate specific to that area
- Neighborhood-relevant phrasing (e.g., for Williamsburg: "Whether you're in a converted warehouse loft or a walk-up on Bedford Ave...")

---

## Pain Points

1. "Is this locksmith actually IN my neighborhood or will they take forever to get here?"
2. "Do they know my building type? (Brownstone, pre-war, condo, etc.)"
3. "I don't want to pay a huge travel surcharge"
4. "Are they trusted by people who live near me?"

## Content Uniqueness Strategy

To avoid thin/duplicate content penalties across 50 similar pages, every page needs:

1. **Unique local intro paragraph** — neighborhood-specific details (mandatory)
2. **Unique FAQ** — at least 2 of 5 questions mention the specific neighborhood
3. **Unique nearby neighborhoods section** — geographically accurate links
4. **Unique map embed** — centered on that specific neighborhood
5. **Unique meta title and description**

The services section can follow a consistent structure but should include the neighborhood name naturally within the content.

## Content Writer Notes
- Research each neighborhood before writing: what is it known for? What building types? What's the demographic? This informs the intro paragraph.
- Brooklyn neighborhoods have STRONG identities — residents are proud of their specific area. Reflect this.
- ZIP codes in the page title and H1 help capture zero-click search features
- Avoid writing 50 identical pages with just the neighborhood name swapped — Google will detect this and it hurts rankings
- Aim for 500–700 words per neighborhood page — enough for substance, not padding
