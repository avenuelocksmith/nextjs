## Who Is Coming to These Pages?

### The Ultra-Specific Emergency Searcher (90%)
This is the highest-intent visitor on the entire website.

- **Who they are:** Someone who is locked out RIGHT NOW and searched specifically for an emergency locksmith in their exact neighborhood
- **Device:** 99% mobile — standing outside their door or car
- **Emotional state:** Stressed, possibly panicked, definitely urgent
- **Query they typed:** "emergency locksmith park slope brooklyn" or "locked out boerum hill" or "locksmith near me dumbo"
- **Time on page:** Under 10 seconds — they see the phone number and call
- **What they need:** Phone number above the fold. Nothing else matters first.
- **What they fear:** Locksmith scams (bait and switch in NYC is a real documented problem)
- **What instantly wins them:** Their specific neighborhood name in the headline + phone number + "30 minutes"

### The Planner (10%)
- Saving a number for future emergencies
- Reads more content, checks reviews
- What wins them: full service info + testimonials

## Page Structure (Template E — Emergency-Optimized)

### CRITICAL: Mobile First Screen
On mobile (375px screen), the FIRST visible content must be:
1. Business name or logo (minimal)
2. H1 — neighborhood-specific, urgent
3. Phone number — LARGE, tap-to-call, styled as a button
4. "30-minute response"
5. "24/7 — Available Right Now"
6. Star rating (trust signal)

No images, no hero background, no auto-playing media that delays the phone number from rendering.

### Hero
- H1 pattern: "Emergency Locksmith in [Neighborhood], Brooklyn — We Arrive in 30 Minutes"
  - Examples:
  - "Emergency Locksmith in Park Slope, Brooklyn — We Arrive in 30 Minutes"
  - "Emergency Locksmith in DUMBO, Brooklyn — Available 24/7"
  - "Emergency Locksmith in Williamsburg, Brooklyn — Locked Out? Call Now."
- Immediate CTA: `[CALL (347) 386-7164 — AVAILABLE NOW]`
- Trust chips (small, fast to load): ⭐ 4.9/5 | 30 Min Response | Licensed & Insured | No Scams — Upfront Pricing

### Reassurance Section (short — under 100 words)
- H2: "Stay Calm — We're On Our Way"
- Calm, reassuring language — acknowledge the stress, promise a solution
- "Avenue Locksmith is a licensed, insured Brooklyn locksmith. We serve [Neighborhood] and will be at your location in under 30 minutes. You'll know the cost before we start work."

### Local Context (50–80 words — unique per neighborhood)
Mention:
- The neighborhood name and surrounding streets
- The types of buildings typical to that neighborhood
- Any relevant local context
- Makes the page feel genuinely local, not mass-produced

**Example for Park Slope:**
> "Park Slope's beautiful brownstones and tree-lined streets are home to thousands of Brooklyn families. Whether you're locked out on 7th Avenue, near Prospect Park, or deeper into the South Slope, Avenue Locksmith knows the area well. We dispatch from Brooklyn and typically arrive to Park Slope within 20 minutes."

### Emergency Services Available
- H2: "Emergency Locksmith Services in [Neighborhood]"
- Apartment/home lockout
- Car lockout (street, garage, parking lot)
- Office/commercial lockout
- Broken key extraction
- Emergency lock replacement (after break-in)
- Emergency rekeying

### Anti-Scam Section (important for NYC market)
- H2: "How to Spot a Legitimate Locksmith — and Why You Can Trust Us"
- NYC has documented locksmith scam operations
- Signs of a scam: no license number, price jumps after arrival, no receipt
- Avenue Locksmith: licensed (#[if available]), always quotes before starting, provides written receipt
- "We are a local Brooklyn business, not a call center that dispatches unknown contractors"

### Process (3 steps — brief)
1. Call (347) 386-7164 — describe your situation
2. We dispatch immediately — arrive in under 30 minutes
3. We solve the problem — you know the price before we start

### Pricing (brief)
- Most emergency lockouts: $75–$200
- Always quoted before work begins
- No hidden fees, no inflated after-hours surcharges

### Testimonials
- 1–2 reviews emphasizing fast response and trustworthiness

### Nearby Neighborhoods
- H2: "Emergency Locksmith Service Near [Neighborhood]"
- 4–5 adjacent neighborhoods with links
- "Can't find your neighborhood? We serve all of Brooklyn."

### FAQ (5 questions)

## Content Writer Notes

### Neighborhoods Context Guide

**Park Slope:** Affluent residential, brownstones, families, Prospect Park adjacent. Professional tone, calm urgency.

**Williamsburg:** Young professionals, converted lofts, L train corridor, mix of new builds and old buildings. Energy matches the neighborhood.

**DUMBO:** High-end condos, artists, tech workers, cobblestone streets, near Brooklyn Bridge. Professional, premium-feel tone.

**Brooklyn Heights:** Historic brownstones, affluent, quiet, families. Reassuring, professional tone.

**Carroll Gardens:** Italian-American roots, brownstones, young families. Warm, community tone.

**Cobble Hill:** Boutique shops, brownstones, strollers and dogs. Community neighbor tone.

**Boerum Hill:** Mix of renovated and original brownstones, transitional neighborhood. Friendly professional.

**Prospect Heights:** Near Barclays Center, Grand Army Plaza, diverse, active. Energetic local tone.

**Vinegar Hill:** Small, quiet, industrial-residential mix. Very specific neighborhood — feel local.

**Mill Basin:** Southeast Brooklyn, residential, waterfront. Community feel.

**Columbia Waterfront District:** Small neighborhood between Red Hook and Cobble Hill. Niche local feel.

### Critical: Do NOT Make These Pages Feel Generated
Each of these 11 pages must feel hand-written for that neighborhood. The local context paragraph (50–80 words) is the most important unique element. Research each neighborhood and write authentically.

### Page Length: Keep It Short
These are EMERGENCY pages. Target 400–500 words of body content. The visitor does not want to read — they want to call. Every additional paragraph below the phone number is a potential distraction.
