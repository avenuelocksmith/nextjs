**Page Goal:** Convert first-time visitors into phone calls or form submissions within 10 seconds.

---

## Who Is Coming to This Page?

The homepage gets three very different visitors. Content must serve all three without confusing any of them.

### Segment 1: The Emergency Visitor (40% of traffic)
- **Who they are:** Someone locked out of their home, car, or office RIGHT NOW in Brooklyn or NYC
- **Age:** 18–65, any demographic
- **Device:** 95% mobile, often in distress, one-handed typing
- **How they got here:** Google search "locksmith near me", "locked out brooklyn", or clicked a Google Business Profile link
- **Time on page:** Under 15 seconds — they scan for a phone number, not content
- **What they need to see immediately:** Phone number in large text, "30-minute response", "available now"
- **Biggest fear:** Getting scammed by a bait-and-switch locksmith
- **What wins them over:** Real phone number (not a tracking redirect), 4.9-star rating visible, "licensed & insured" text

### Segment 2: The Comparison Shopper (30% of traffic)
- **Who they are:** Brooklyn resident or business owner who isn't locked out right now but needs a locksmith soon (lock upgrade, new apartment move-in, lost keys, commercial access control)
- **Age:** 25–55
- **Device:** 50% mobile, 50% desktop
- **How they got here:** Google search, neighbor referral, Google Business Profile
- **Time on page:** 30–90 seconds — reading reviews, checking services
- **What they need to see:** Services menu, reviews/rating, service area, pricing range, years in business
- **Biggest fear:** Hiring someone unreliable or overpriced
- **What wins them over:** Transparent pricing mention ($125–$700), 150 reviews, "licensed, bonded, insured" badge

### Segment 3: The Referral / Brand Visitor (30% of traffic)
- **Who they are:** Someone told them to call Avenue Locksmith, or they've used the service before
- **Device:** Mixed
- **How they got here:** Direct URL, Google "avenue locksmith brooklyn"
- **Time on page:** Under 30 seconds — looking for phone number or contact form
- **What they need:** Fast path to phone/contact
- **What wins them over:** They already trust the brand — just need easy contact

## Above-the-Fold Requirements (Mobile)

The first screen on a mobile phone must contain:
- [ ] Business name visible
- [ ] H1 with primary keyword ("Locksmith in Brooklyn, NY" or similar)
- [ ] Phone number as a `tel:` link — large tap target (min 48px height)
- [ ] "Available 24/7 — Under 30 Min Response"
- [ ] Star rating + review count (4.9/5 · 150+ reviews)
- [ ] Single secondary CTA ("View Services" or "Get a Free Quote")

## Do Not Do
- Do not bury the phone number below the fold on mobile
- Do not use a hero image that slows LCP above 2.5s
- Do not use vague hero text like "Your Security is Our Priority" without specifics
- Do not list services without linking to their individual pages
