## Who Is Coming to This Page?

### Segment 1: Locked Out of Their Car RIGHT NOW (55%)
- **Who they are:** Driver in Brooklyn or NYC, keys locked inside the car, possibly on a busy street
- **Age:** 18–65
- **Device:** 100% mobile — often typing with one hand standing next to their car
- **Emotional state:** Frustrated, stressed, possibly late for work or an appointment
- **Urgency:** MAXIMUM — they need someone NOW
- **Pain:** "I'm stuck. How fast can someone get here? Is this going to cost a fortune?"
- **Decision driver:** Response time, price clarity, "can you handle my car make/model"
- **What wins them:** Large phone number, "30 minutes", "we handle all makes and models", no-nonsense price range

### Segment 2: Lost / Broken Car Key (25%)
- **Who they are:** Lost their car key, broke their key fob, or need a spare cut
- **Urgency:** High but not instant emergency — might be planning ahead
- **Device:** Mix of mobile and desktop
- **Pain:** "Dealerships want $300-500 for a key. Is there a cheaper option?"
- **Decision driver:** Significantly cheaper than dealership, can program transponder/fob correctly
- **What wins them:** Price comparison to dealer, "we program all key types", same-day service

### Segment 3: Ignition Problem (15%)
- **Who they are:** Key won't turn, key stuck in ignition, ignition replaced on vehicle
- **Urgency:** High — car is disabled
- **Device:** Mobile
- **Pain:** "Is this a locksmith job or a mechanic job? I'm not sure who to call."
- **Decision driver:** Clarity that locksmiths handle ignition issues, expertise signal, response time
- **What wins them:** Clear "yes we handle ignition repair" messaging, explanation of what we do

### Segment 4: The Planner / Research Phase (5%)
- **Who they are:** Saving a number "just in case", or a new car owner wanting to get a spare key made proactively
- **Urgency:** Low
- **Device:** Desktop
- **What wins them:** Full service explanation, pricing, reviews
